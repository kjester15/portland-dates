In The City Hikes

In The City

Powell's City of Books

  • Powell's City of Books is the largest used and new bookstore in the world, occupying an entire city block and housing approximately one million books.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but extra nice on a rainy day.
  • Tip: Start at the cafe with a warm beverage and walk around the store. Don’t forget to check out the rare book room! It’s also a great place to do some gift shopping.

Mill Ends Park

  • The world's smallest park, as featured in the Guinness Book of World Records, consists of one tree and some seasonal plantings.
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: Pair it with a walk and shop around Pioneer Courthouse Square!

Christmas Ships Parade

  • Averaging 60 ships in the parade, this annual Portland tradition packs restaurants and hotels along the Columbia and the Willamette. By the thousands every night regardless of the weather Portland-Vancouver residents line the banks of both rivers to watch the parade.
  • Best time to go: December, on a clear parade night.
  • Tip: Pack tea or a hot toddy to stay warm for your viewing pleasure.

Portland Winter Lights Festival

  • This annual event aims to bring light to Portland in the winter. It consists of many art installations and events throughout the city.
  • Best time to go: February.
  • Tip: There’s a silent disco! Check the website to find out this year's schedule.

Hikes

Mount Tabor Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 10 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round!
  • Mileage: 2
  • Tip: In summer, bring a picnic blanket and stay a while. In winter, it’s a perfect hike to move your legs – grab food or drink nearby to make this an extra nice outing!

Oaks Bottom

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 3
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: A perfect winter hike to move your legs. Grab food or drink in Sellwood to make this an extra nice outing!

Powell Butte

  • Drive time from Portland: 20 minutes
  • Mileage: 6
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a clear day.
  • Tip: This exposed trail has beautiful views of the mountains. Perfect for a summer sunset, or a nice day in summer, fall, or winter.

Latourell, Shepherds Dell, and Bridal Veil Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 2
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but less busy in winter.
  • Tip: Hike the Latourell Falls and then stop by these other falls nearby as they are a short walk and not worth the drive on their own.

Oxbow Park

  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Mileage: 7.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: If you want a shorter hike, do the Sandy River trail (4 miles).

Milo McIver State Park

  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but perfect for a winter escape! On a clear day, there are views of Mount Hood.
  • Tip: Pair with a stop in Estacada!

Wahclella Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Mileage: 2
  • Best time to go: Year-round. In summer it gets crowded as people swim here!

Dry Creek Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 45 minutes
  • Mileage: 4.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: Stop in Cascade Locks for a bite after this hike!

Moulton Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 50 minutes
  • Mileage: 5.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round. In summer it’s very crowded as this is a popular swimming spot.

Warrior Rock on Sauvie Island

  • Drive time from Portland: 50 minutes
  • Mileage: 6
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: This trail is longer, but it’s flat so it’s easy! Pair with a stop in Sauvie Island or Collins Beach. Make sure to stop for a parking pass online or at the island - there’s nothing at the trailhead.

Trail of Ten Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 7.5
  • Best time to go: Spring or Winter for the heaviest water flow. Fall for amazing fall colors. Summer is crowded and the worst season to visit this magical place.

Indian Beach, Ecola State Park

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 3.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, can get really muddy if it’s rained a lot recently.
  • Tip: Hike to Crescent Beach (2.5 miles) for an additional hike. Stay for sunset and watch from the overlook at the parking lot. Pair with a trip to Cannon Beach!

Drift Creek Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours 10 minutes
  • Mileage: 3
  • Best time to go: Winter and Spring for the heaviest water flow.
  • Tip: Pair with a trip to Lincoln City for a weekend trip!

Cape Disappointment

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 2
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but in winter, the Kings Tides are amazing here.
  • Tip: Don't let it's name fool you! Make sure to go down to Deadman’s Cove for an amazing viewpoint. Watch the sunset here! Since it’s a long drive, why not camp or pair it with a weekend trip to Long Beach, WA or Astoria, OR.

Snowshoe

Silver Star Mountain

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour
  • Mileage: 6.5
  • Tip: Go on a clear day that’s not windy, and bring lots of snacks because it’s killer!

Trillium Lake

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour
  • Mileage: 5
  • Tip: Go on a clear day!

Bennett Pass

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Tip: Continue to the end for great views of Mount Hood.

Tamanawas Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 3.5
  • Tip: Since it’s a shorter hike, make the most of your trip and stop in Hood River on your way home! You also likely only need microspikes for this hike as it sees a lot of traffic.

Summit Mount Saint Helens

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 10.5
  • Tip: Many say it’s easier in winter than in summer! But you definitely want to go on a day with nice weather. Camp nearby for an easier, earlier start.

High Hut

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hour 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 8
  • Tip: Go on a clear day for incredible views.
