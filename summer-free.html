In The City

Flicks on the Bricks

  • Portland’s original FREE outdoor movie series returns each summer to Portland’s Living Room, where the Square is transformed into Portland’s largest outdoor movie theater for all community members to enjoy!
  • Best time to go: This event takes place in July.

Portland’s Waterfront Blues Festival

  • Held in Tom McCall Waterfront Park with views of Portland’s famous bridges and riverfront, the festival is the perfect way to celebrate the Fourth of July Weekend. With family-friendly activities, sunny skies, the world-famous Zydeco dance floor, and fireworks over the Willamette River.
  • Best time to go: This event takes place in July.

Cathedral Park Jazz Festival

  • The Jazz Society’s Cathedral Park Jazz Festival is a free concert open to the public and held over three days in mid-July at Cathedral Park at the base of the St. Johns Bridge.
  • Best time to go: This event takes place in July.

Watch PDX Adult Soap Box Derby

  • Once a year Mount Tabor park becomes a whacky racetrack. Bringing around 10,000 visitors per year this is an event not to miss.
  • Best time to go: This event takes place in August.
  • Tip: Get coffee or food nearby and then go to the park early to secure a parking and watching spot.

Portland Night Market

  • 175+ of Portland's best vendors come together on Friday and Saturday evenings throughout the year celebrating local food, culture, music, drinks and retail.
  • Best time to go: Year-round! But summer for warmth in the evening.
  • Tip: If you go in the winter, go to Smith Teamaker before for a flight of hot tea to get you ready for an evening outside!

Portland Naked Bike Ride

  • Helmet on for safety, clothes off for fun. Join Portland’s World Naked Bike Ride – the largest one in the world – to protest against dependency on oil.
  • Best time to go: It happens in June every year!

Shakespear In The Park

  • See a free Shakespear play in a park, bring snacks and drinks for a perfect and unique and utterly Portland date.
  • Best time to go: Throughout summer.
  • Tip: This happens at parks throughout Portland so check the summer schedule to find a play at a park near you!

Disc Golf at Pier Park

  • Oregon is home to nearly 200 disc golf courses, and a much loved one in the Portland Metro. With new disc golfs costing only about $20 this is an easy access, year-round activity.
  • Best time to go: This park is great in every season, but summer is extra nice.
  • Tip: Get dinner or drinks in the St Johns neighborhood after (or before)!

First Thursday Art Walk in the Pearl District

  • On the first Thursday of each month walk the streets of the Pearl district with many others to see artists galleries and shop locally.
  • Best time to go: This event runs April-October, but summer always guarantees nice weather.
  • Tip: Parking in the Pearl district can be hard, so park on the East side and walk across the Broadway Bridge.

Last Thursday Art Walk in the Alberta Arts District

  • Not as busy as the Art Walks in the Pearl district, this is a great option for a more chill way to browse local goods.
  • Best time to go: This even is year-round, but outdoor performers tend to be there May-September.
  • Tip: In summer, there’s often live music and dancing!

Bike the Marine Drive Trail

  • The paved Marine Drive trail has sections right along the river with view of Mount Hood. It’s 10 miles roundtrip!
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a clear day.
  • Tip: Go for sunset in the summer to avoid crowds, or go on nice days in the off season.

Play tennis at your local park

  • Portland is covered in tennis courts! A great summer activity is picking a park near you and having an evening tennis sweat sesh.

Have a picnic or watch the sunset at one of these locations...!

Laurelhurst Park

  • Take a walk or have a picnic in this cute park. Extend it by wandering around the cute neighborhood or walking to nearby food and drink.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a dry day.
  • Tip: In the summertime, there’s often free live comedy and silent disco here!

Cathedral Park

  • Have a picnic under this iconic bridge, walk across it, and go on a hike in Forest Park, or check out the St Johns neighborhood for food and drink.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a dry day.
  • Tip: In the summertime, there’s a free Jazz Festival here! There’s also other live music and events that happen, so keep an eye out.

Pittock Mansion

  • The views from Pittock Mansion offer a scene of downtown Portland with the backdrop of Mount Hood.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a dry day.
  • Tip: If you have friends who enjoy history in town, take a tour of the mansion.

Mount Tabor

  • Walk a few miles before settling down to watch the sunset in this SE Portland Park.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a dry day.
  • Tip: There are often DJs and dancing on Wednesdays!

Council Crest Park

  • Council Crest Park in SW Portland is thought to be the highest point in Portland. With views of Mount Hood and Mount Saint Helens, this park would make a great picnic spot for sunset.
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a clear day.
  • Tip: Don’t park in the parking lot if you plan to go for sunset as the gates close! Park in the neighborhood and walk.

Mocks Crest Park

  • A perfect spot to be in NE Portland for sunset! Join many others in drinking wine, eating charcuterie, playing games, and soaking up the sun.
  • Best time to go: Summer for a sunset picnic.
  • Tip: Get there early to secure a good spot and enjoy extra time outside.

Rocky Butte

  • Watch the sunset or do a short hike here on a clear day for amazing views of Mount Hood and Mount Saint Helens.
  • Best time to go: Year-round on a clear day.
  • Tip: Go during the week to avoid crowds!

Go swimming!

Poet's Beach

  • Grab your towels and floaty devices and head to the west side for a beach in the city.
  • Best time to go: Summer for swimming.
  • Tip: Go after work on hot days to avoid crowds.

Elk Rock Island

  • Elk Rock is a wondering “hidden gem” of Portland that offers swimming and walking, hidden in a SE Suburb.
  • Best time to go: Summer for swimming.
  • Tip: Get there early to secure a spot on the sand instead of rocks. Walk around as there are many options to post up.

Sellwood Riverfront Park

  • Another great swim spot in SE Portland!
  • Best time to go: Summer for swimming.
  • Tip: It’s dog friendly so bring your pup for some summer fun.

Broughton Beach

  • This beach near the airport is wide and long so you’re guaranteed to find a spot to set up a blanket.
  • Best time to go: Summer for swimming, but NE Marine Drive is a great place to walk or bike year-round.
  • Tip: Go after work to avoid weekend family crowds!

Kelley Point Park

  • This is the northernmost park in Portland! This park is less crowded due to the strong undercurrents where the Willamette and the Columbia.
  • Best time to go: Year-round on a sunny day, bring your dog!
  • Tip: It’s recommended to mostly sun on the beach and go in the shallow part of the water. Go for a long time on the weekend to relax!

Further Out

Lacamas Lake Park

  • SUP, paddle, swim, or hike!
  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: Check out the cute and quaint downtown Camas on this excursion.

Watch the sunset at Vista House

  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round.

Watch the sunset at Women's Forum State Scenic Viewpoint

  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round.

Watch the sunset at Jonsrud Viewpoint

  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a clear day.

Battle Ground Lake

  • SUP, paddle, swim, or hike!
  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: You can camp here, or stop for a dinner in Battle Ground after!

Bike the Banks-Vernonia State Trail

  • Banks-Vernonia State Trail is the first “rails-to-trails” park in Oregon. The trail follows an abandoned railroad bed that stretches 21 miles between the cities of Banks and Vernonia.
  • Drive time from Portland: 40 minutes
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but summer and fall are particularly nice.
  • Tip: Go during the week to avoid crowds!

Trillium Lake

  • Rent a kayak and go out on this gorgeous lake backdropped by Mount Hood.
  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour
  • Best time to go: Year-round on clear days, but be prepared for a snowshoe in winter.
  • Tip: Note that this place is a paradise for families, so you won’t be alone. Simply watch the sunset during the week to avoid the crowds.

Lost Lake

  • Rent a kayak or SUP, or simply watch the sunset on this gorgeous lake backdropped by Mount Hood.
  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 50 minutes
  • Best time to go: Summer and fall.
  • Tip: Since it’s a little farther than Trillium Lake, it typically isn’t as crowded.
