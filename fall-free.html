In The City Further Out Hikes

In The City

Swifts at Chapman Elementary School

  • Every year in September around 16,000 migrating Vaux’s Swift circle overhead at sunset as they prepare to roost for the night. This population has been returning to Chapman Elementary Schools Chimney since the 1980s and is one of the largest known roosting sites of migrating Vaux’s Swifts. Stay until the last swift goes in the chimney and cheer along with everyone else.
  • Best time to go: September.
  • Tip: Arrive at least 1.5 hours before sunset to secure a good spot and enjoy a picnic dinner.

Walk the East Bank Esplanade

  • Enjoy fall colors along Portland’s waterfront. Walk as long as you like!
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: This view of Hawthorn Bridge is gorgeous in the fall!

West Coast Giant Pumpkin Regatta

  • Don’t miss out on one of Oregon’s most unique Fall festivals. Since 2004, People have been coming to Oregon to climb inside gigantic pumpkins each October and paddle them in a one-of-a-kind race across a lake.
  • Best time to go: This event takes place in October.

Further Out

Cedar Grist Mill

  • Walk around the Cedar Grist Mill and take photos of the Fall foliage. Tour the mill to see how millers milled grain the way it was done in 1876. Don’t forget to take home a bag of fresh flour or cornmeal. It’s open to visitors on the weekend! Get there early to beat the crowds.
  • Drive time from Portland: 50 minutes
  • Best time to go: Fall.
  • Tip: Pair with a walk around Salmon Creek Trail to stretch your legs and get some wildlife viewing in!

Go foraging for chanterelles

  • Sign up for a mushroom foraging meetup for some local tips, or take your stab at it in Tillamook State Forest, Grifford-Pinchot National Forest, or Mount Hood National Forest. Make sure to go after some rain!
  • Best time to go: Spring and Fall.
  • Tip: Leave the trail and make your own adventure for the best mushroom finding.

Hikes

Lower Macleay Park to Pittock Mansion

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, on a clear day. Fall is particularly nice due to the foliage in Forest Park!
  • Tip: Hike past Witches Castle and see a great view of Portland! Option to take a paid tour of Pittock Mansion when you’re there.

Marquam, Towhee, Flicker & Warble Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 4
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: Explore other trails too, this loop trail is just a good one!

Maple and Wildwood Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 7.6
  • Best time to go: Year-round.

Wildwood and Redwood Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 2.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round.

Forest Park Ridge Trail

  • Drive time from Portland: 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: This trail has beautiful views of the St Johns Bridge! Get food in the neighborhood before or after your hike.

Lacamas Creek Park Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 3
  • Best time to go: Year-round.
  • Tip: In summer, take a dip or a SUP. In fall, the colors here are gorgeous! Pair this with a trip to downtown Camas for some food.

Multnomah-Wahkeena Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 30 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, each season has its own beauty. Fall colors are beautiful at Multnomah Falls.
  • Tip: Go after work to avoid weekend crowds.

Cape Horn Loop

  • Drive time from Portland: 45 minutes
  • Mileage: 6.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but there is some Fall foliage on this trail.

Ramona Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 8
  • Best time to go: Fall, due to easy stream crossing.
  • Tip: Make this a loop trail and get a permit online to look for chanterelles while you're in the area!

Bald Mountain from Lolo Pass

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 6.5
  • Best time to go: Fall, for fall colors, late spring would also be lovely before the biting flies are out.
  • Tip: Combine this with a trip to Lost Lake! Lolo Pass road has amazing views of Mount Hood.

Abiqua Falls

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 1
  • Best time to go: Late summer and fall as the trail can get flooded.
  • Tip: Download GPS as it can be difficult to find. Bring a picnic as it’s a long drive for a short hike – but oh so worth it!

Salmon River Trail

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 7.5
  • Best time to go: Year-round, but fall is extra nice for the foliage!

Cape Falcon

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 40 minutes
  • Mileage: 5
  • Best time to go: Summer and Fall as it is incredibly muddy other times of the year.
  • Tip: Pair this hike with other coastal hikes and a camping trip!

Neahkanie Mountain

  • Drive time from Portland: 1 hour 45 minutes
  • Mileage: 2.5
  • Best time to go: Accessible year-round, but Fall will have a dry trail which is good for this steep hike, and less crowded than in Summer.
  • Tip: Pair this hike with other coastal hikes and a camping trip!

Indian Racetrack and Red Mountain Fire Lookout

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours
  • Mileage: 6.5
  • Best time to go: Late summer and fall for no mosquitos.
  • Tip: You can actually drive up to this lookout in a high-clearance vehicle, and there is plenty of dispersed camping in this area!

Lookout Mountain

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours
  • Best time to go: Summer and fall for gorgeous colors.

Indian Haven Wilderness Trail #33

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Mileage: 6
  • Best time to go: Fall.
  • Tip: Indian Haven wilderness is a wonderful place to be in the Fall! The mosquitoes are gone and people don’t crowd to it. Spend time camping and exploring nearby areas such as Panther Creek Falls.

Flag Point Lookout

  • Drive time from Portland: 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Best time to go: September or October.
  • Tip: Badger Creek Wilderness is the only area in Oregon’s Cascades that sees Western Larches!
